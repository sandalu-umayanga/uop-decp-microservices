## 1. Introduction

Welcome to **UniConnect**! As an **Alumni** member, you play a vital role in bridging the gap between academia and industry. UniConnect empowers you to give back to your department by sharing professional insights, posting career opportunities, and mentoring the next generation of graduates.

### What You Can Do as Alumni

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Feed** | Share professional updates, department news, and career advice |
| **Career Posting** | Post jobs and internships from your company; review student applications |
| **Profile** | Maintain a professional profile showcasing your department history |
| **Networking** | Connect with current students, fellow alumni, and administrators |

### Key Difference from Students

| Capability | Student | Alumni |
|------------|---------|--------|
| Create feed posts | ✅ | ✅ |
| Apply for jobs | ✅ | ❌ |
| Post jobs/internships | ❌ | ✅ |
| View applicants | ❌ | ✅ (own postings) |
| Like & comment | ✅ | ✅ |

## 2. Getting Started

### 2.1 System Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Browser** | Google Chrome 90+, Firefox 90+, Safari 14+, or Edge 90+ |
| **Screen** | Fully responsive — works on phones (320px+), tablets, and desktops |
| **Network** | Stable internet connection or same local network as the server |

### 2.2 Accessing the Platform

1. Open your web browser.
2. Navigate to the UniConnect URL:
   - **Local**: `http://localhost`
   - **Cloud**: `https://<your-domain>`
3. You will see the Login page.

## 3. Account Management

### 3.1 Creating Your Account

1. On the Login page, click **"Don't have an account? Register"**.
2. Fill in the registration form:

   | Field | Description | Example |
   |-------|-------------|---------|
   | **Full Name** | Your professional display name | `Nimali Fernando` |
   | **Email** | A valid email address | `nimali.f@alumni.pdn.ac.lk` |
   | **Password** | Minimum 6 characters recommended | `••••••••` |
   | **Role** | Select **ALUMNI** | `ALUMNI` |

3. Click **Create Account**.
4. On success, proceed to the Login page.

> **Tip**: Use a recognisable name and professional email so that current students can identify you.

### 3.2 Logging In

1. Enter your **email** and **password**.
2. Click **Sign in**.
3. You are redirected to the Feed page.
4. Your session lasts **24 hours**.

### 3.3 Logging Out

1. Click the **Logout** button (top-right on desktop; inside the hamburger menu on mobile).
2. Your token is removed and you are redirected to the Login page.

## 4. Your Profile

### 4.1 Viewing Your Profile

Click **Profile** in the navigation bar. Your profile displays:

- **Avatar** — First letter of your name in a coloured badge
- **Name** — Your display name
- **Department** — e.g., Computer Engineering
- **Graduation Year** — When you graduated
- **Biography** — Professional summary visible to all members
- **Connections** — Number of connections

### 4.2 Editing Your Profile

1. Click **Edit Profile** on your profile page.
2. Update any of the following:

   | Field | Suggestion for Alumni |
   |-------|----------------------|
   | **Display Name** | Use your full professional name |
   | **Department** | Your original department at the university |
   | **Graduation Year** | Year you completed your degree |
   | **Short Bio** | Current role, company, industry experience, areas of expertise |

3. Click **Save Changes**.

> **Example Bio**: *"Class of 2020, Computer Engineering. Currently a Senior Software Engineer at WSO2. Passionate about open-source middleware and cloud-native architecture. Happy to mentor students interested in distributed systems."*

### 4.3 Profile Analytics

The sidebar shows:

| Metric | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Profile Views** | How many members viewed your profile |
| **Professional Connections** | Your total connections on the platform |

## 5. Department Feed

### 5.1 Browsing the Feed

1. Click **Feed** in the navigation bar.
2. Posts appear in reverse chronological order.
3. Desktop layout: Three-column view with your stats on the left, the feed in the centre, and trending content on the right.

### 5.2 Creating a Post

1. Type your update in the **"What's on your mind?"** text area at the top of the feed.
2. Click **Post**.
3. Your post instantly appears at the top.

**Ideas for Alumni Posts**:
- Share career advice and industry trends
- Announce open positions at your company
- Share memories or tips about university life
- Celebrate professional milestones (promotions, publications)
- Offer mentoring or AMA (Ask Me Anything) sessions

### 5.3 Liking Posts

- Click **👍** to like a post; it toggles to **❤️**.
- Click again to unlike.

### 5.4 Commenting & Replying

1. Click **💬** on a post to expand comments.
2. Type your comment and click **Post** (or press **Enter**).
3. Use the **Reply** link on any comment for threaded discussions.

### 5.5 Managing Your Content

- **Delete your post**: Click the **Delete** button on your own posts.
- **Edit/Delete comments**: Use the **Edit** or **Delete** options on your own comments.
- You **cannot** modify other users' content.

## 6. Career & Opportunities — Posting Jobs

This is one of your most impactful capabilities as Alumni. You can post jobs and internships for current students and review their applications.

### 6.1 Browsing Existing Listings

1. Click **Careers** in the navigation bar.
2. All active job and internship postings are displayed as cards.
3. Each card shows: Title, Company, Type (JOB/INTERNSHIP), Description, and Posted Date.

### 6.2 Creating a Job or Internship Posting

1. At the top of the Careers page, you will find the **"Post a New Opportunity"** form.
2. Fill in the required fields:

   | Field | Description | Example |
   |-------|-------------|---------|
   | **Job Title** | Clear, specific title | `Backend Engineer Intern` |
   | **Company** | Your organisation name | `WSO2 Lanka (Pvt) Ltd` |
   | **Description** | Detailed description of the role, requirements, and benefits | `We are looking for…` |

3. Click **Post Job**.
4. Your listing immediately appears in the Careers feed for all members.

**Best Practices for Job Postings**:

| Do ✅ | Don't ❌ |
|-------|----------|
| Include required skills and qualifications | Post vague or incomplete descriptions |
| Mention location (remote/on-site/hybrid) | Forget to include the company name |
| Specify application deadline if any | Post duplicate listings |
| Provide contact information or next steps | Use misleading job titles |

### 6.3 Viewing Applicants (ATS Dashboard)

Once students start applying to your posted jobs:

1. On your job listing card, a **View Applicants** button appears.
2. Click it to open the **Applicant Tracking System (ATS)** dashboard.
3. You can see:

   | Column | Description |
   |--------|-------------|
   | **Applicant Name** | The student who applied |
   | **Cover Letter** | Their personalised application message |
   | **Resume URL** | Link to their resume/portfolio (clickable) |
   | **Status** | Current status (SUBMITTED, REVIEWED, etc.) |
   | **Submitted Date** | When they applied |

4. Review applications and take appropriate action through your company's hiring process.

### 6.4 Managing Your Listings

- You can manage job postings that you created.
- To remove an expired or filled position, use the delete option on your job card.

## 8. Mentoring Students — Best Practices

As an alumni, you are uniquely positioned to guide current students. Here are recommendations for making the most of UniConnect:

### On the Feed
- **Answer questions**: When students post about career confusion or technical doubts, share your experience.
- **Share resources**: Post links to useful courses, books, or tools.
- **Celebrate achievements**: Like and comment on student posts to encourage engagement.

### On Careers
- **Post real opportunities**: Use the career module to share genuine openings at your company.
- **Provide feedback**: After reviewing applications, consider reaching out to promising candidates.
- **Be responsive**: Students value timely communication about their applications.

### On Networking
- **Accept connections**: Growing your network helps students see they have access to alumni support.
- **Complete your profile**: A detailed bio helps students understand your expertise and reach out for relevant advice.

| Device | Navigation | Layout |

- The menu closes automatically on page navigation.

## 10. Security & Privacy

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Password** | BCrypt-hashed; never stored in plain text |
| **Session** | JWT tokens expire after 24 hours |
| **Ownership** | Only you can edit/delete your own content |
| **Applicant Data** | Student applications (cover letters, resumes) are only visible to the job poster |

### Recommendations
- Use a strong, unique password.
- Log out on shared devices.
- Do not share login credentials.
- Be mindful of sensitive information in job postings.

## 11. Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Cannot log in | Verify email and password; ensure you registered as ALUMNI |
| Job posting form not visible | Only Alumni and Admin roles can see the posting form |
| Cannot see applicants | The "View Applicants" button appears only on jobs **you** posted |
| Page shows loading spinner indefinitely | Refresh the page; check network; contact administrator if persistence |
| Mobile menu doesn't respond | Hard-refresh (Ctrl+Shift+R on desktop, pull-to-refresh on mobile) |
| Post not appearing | Check internet; try refreshing; ensure the post was submitted |

## 12. FAQ

**Q: Can I apply for jobs as an Alumni?**
A: No. The apply feature is exclusive to the Student role. As Alumni, you can post and manage job listings.

**Q: Can I change my role to Student or Admin?**
A: Role changes can only be performed by an Admin user. Contact your platform administrator.

**Q: Can I see who viewed my profile?**
A: You can see the total number of profile views, but individual viewer identities are not shown.

**Q: Is there a limit to how many jobs I can post?**
A: There is no hard limit. Post responsibly and remove expired listings.

**Q: Can I edit a job after posting?**
A: Currently, you can delete and re-create a listing. In-place editing is planned for a future release.

**Q: How do I handle a student's application?**
A: View their cover letter and resume URL through the ATS dashboard on your job card. Follow up with shortlisted candidates through your company's hiring process.

**Q: Can other users see the applicants for my job?**
A: No. Applicant data is private and visible only to the user who posted the job.
